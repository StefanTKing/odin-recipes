Lasagna

Description

In the heart of traditional Italian cuisine lies a dish revered by many but mastered by few: lasagna. This layered marvel, with its origins dating back to Ancient Rome, has become a symbol of comfort food worldwide. Each bite encapsulates a harmonious blend of flavors, telling tales of old family gatherings, long Sunday afternoons, and the warmth of shared meals.

Our lasagna recipe, passed down through generations, boasts a rich meat sauce, delicate homemade pasta sheets, and a creamy béchamel sauce, all generously interspersed with molten cheese. The end result is not just a dish but an experience — a culinary journey that transports you straight to a rustic Italian kitchen where every ingredient, every layer, tells its own delicious story. Prepare yourself for an authentic taste of Italy, right from your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • Homemade pasta sheets or store-bought lasagna sheets
  • 500g ground beef or a mix of beef and pork
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 800g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 500ml béchamel sauce
  • 250g ricotta cheese
  • 200g mozzarella cheese, grated
  • 50g parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and minced garlic. Sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the ground beef to the pan and cook until browned. Break apart any large chunks as it cooks.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, and dried basil. Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. While the sauce is simmering, prepare the béchamel sauce in another pot. Once done, set aside.
  6. In a large baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce at the bottom. This will prevent the pasta from sticking.
  7. Place a layer of pasta sheets over the sauce. If you're using store-bought sheets that require pre-cooking, make sure they're boiled and drained first.
  8. Spread a portion of ricotta cheese over the pasta, followed by a layer of béchamel sauce.
  9. Sprinkle a generous amount of grated mozzarella and parmesan cheese.
  10. Repeat the layers – meat sauce, pasta sheets, ricotta, béchamel, mozzarella, and parmesan – until all ingredients are used up, finishing with a layer of cheese on top.
  11. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
  12. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and continue to bake for another 10-15 minutes or until the top layer is golden brown and bubbly.
  13. Once baked, remove from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes to set.
  14. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving. Enjoy your authentic Italian lasagna!
